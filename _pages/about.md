    <h3>Can LLMs Be One of Us?</h3>
    <p>
      This project explores whether LLMs can participate meaningfully in human group conversations‚Äînot just by generating relevant content, but by displaying socially appropriate interactional behaviors.
      We examine how GPT adapts to different roles across formal, semi-formal, and informal settings. The study draws on Michael Silverstein‚Äôs theories of <em>interactional textuality</em> and <em>indexicality</em> to analyze how AI uses language to manage turn-taking, respond to challenges, repair ambiguity, and negotiate social norms.
      This simulation-based framework offers new insights into AI‚Äôs capacity for constructing identity and trust in multi-party interactions.
